Scientist Focus: V. Kelly Turner, Ph.D.

Dr. Turner is an Associate Professor of Urban Planning and Geography at UCLA, where she also serves as the Associate Director of the Luskin Center for Innovation. Her highly interdisciplinary research draws from social-ecological systems to address urban planning and environmental problem domains.

Her work on the Urban Heat Island (UHI) effect is a cornerstone of her career, focusing on providing evidence-based policy and planning solutions to protect vulnerable populations from extreme heat. She is a critical voice in the environmental justice space, using her geographic and climate data expertise to expose the sociohistorical roots of heat inequality, specifically showing how racist policies like redlining continue to result in higher temperatures and lower tree canopy cover in marginalized communities today.

Key Contributions to UHI and Education:

  • Pioneering the concept of "shade infrastructure" (both trees and built elements) as a primary and immediate defense against heat exposure.
  • Investigating heat governance—the policies and institutions that mitigate heat—and ensuring climate adaptation planning is integrated into city policy.
  • Researching the disproportionate impacts of heat on students and schools, emphasizing that cooling resources are a basic need for educational equity.
